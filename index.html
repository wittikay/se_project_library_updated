Reader picks

That our readers love

  • Hatchet

    Brian Robeson, a thirteen-year-old boy, survives a plane crash in the Canadian wilderness. With only a hatchet, a gift from his mother, he learns to fend for himself. Over 54 days, he faces wild animals, harsh weather, and his own fears, ultimately discovering his inner strength and resilience.

    Gary Paulsen

  • Holes

    Stanley Yelnats is sent to Camp Green Lake, a juvenile detention center, for a crime he didn’t commit. The boys there are forced to dig holes every day, but Stanley soon realizes the warden is searching for a hidden treasure. As he digs, he uncovers secrets about his family’s past and breaks a family curse.

    Louis Sachar

  • My Side of the Mountain

    Sam Gribley leaves his crowded New York City apartment to live in the Catskill Mountains. Seeking independence, he survives by building a home in a hollow tree, foraging for food, and taming a falcon named Frightful. Through his adventure, Sam learns valuable lessons about self-reliance and the importance of companionship.

    Jean Craighead George

  • The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People

    Stephen Covey’s guide to personal and professional success outlines seven habits: be proactive, begin with the end in mind, put first things first, think win-win, seek first to understand, then to be understood, synergize, and sharpen the saw. The book emphasizes a principle-centered approach, focusing on character development and long-term effectiveness. This book resonates with me closely as it taught me many practices I use to this day. I would reccomend this to anybody who wishes to increase their effectiveness in every aspect of their life.

    Stephen Covey

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.
